Fix \(y \in \X\) and treat \(k(x,y)\) as a univariate function of \(x \in \X\).
By the reproducing property, \(k(x,y) = \ipt{k(x,\cdot),k(y,\cdot)}\).
Define \(\Phi(x) = k(x,\cdot)\) to give the desired result in \cref{eqn:kernel-inner-product-1}.
